Vyagghapajja Sutta: 4 Factors for Worldly Progress

As preached to Dighajanu, there are four main principles a layperson should follow to become economically stable.

💪

Utthana Sampada

Earning through Effort

Doing one's job or business with enthusiasm, without laziness. It means earning wealth righteously and legally through sweat and hard work.

🔐

Arakkha Sampada

Protecting Earned Wealth

Protecting the earned wealth from thieves, fire, water, and unworthy heirs. Minimizing unnecessary expenses and waste is a key component here.

🤝

Kalyana Mittata

Good Companionship

Associating with virtuous friends who support your progress and do not lead you to gambling or sin. Wealth can be destroyed because of bad friends.

⚖️

Samajivikata

Balanced Livelihood

Spending according to one's income. Spending more than the income leads to debt. Buddhism also does not approve of being miserly by spending too little.

Anana Sutta: True Financial Happiness

The Buddha pointed out four types of happiness a layperson can enjoy. The ultimate goal of earning and spending money is to achieve this mental well-being. Click the buttons below.

💰

Atthi Sukha (Happiness of Ownership)

The mental happiness gained by thinking, "I have wealth earned righteously and through my own effort." Having enough assets to use when needed brings great mental relief.

🍽️

Bhoga Sukha (Happiness of Enjoying Wealth)

The joy of using earned wealth to support oneself, feed one's family, and help others (doing good deeds). Buddhism values utilizing wealth rather than just hoarding it.

🕊️

Anana Sukha (Happiness of Being Debt-Free)

The clear happiness of thinking, "I owe no debt, small or large, to anyone." Living in debt is shown as a great sorrow in Buddhism. This happiness can be achieved through balanced living (Samajivikata).

✨

Anavajja Sukha (Happiness of Blamelessness)

The flawless happiness felt in the mind because the way wealth is earned and spent is legal and ethical. This is the highest mental well-being, free from wrong livelihoods like fraud, deceit, or killing animals.
